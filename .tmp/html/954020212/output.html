Problem:

What is \(7 \sqrt{3}\) minus \(3 \sqrt{3}\)? Return your answer in the simplest form.

Solution:

To solve \(7 \sqrt{3} - 3 \sqrt{3}\), follow these steps:

  1. Notice that both terms contain the common factor \(\sqrt{3}\).
  2. Factor out the common factor \(\sqrt{3}\) from both terms.

Let's rewrite the expression for clarity:

\[7 \sqrt{3} - 3 \sqrt{3}\]

Factor out \(\sqrt{3}\):

\[ 7 \sqrt{3} - 3 \sqrt{3} = (7 - 3) \sqrt{3} \]

Now, subtract the coefficients \(7\) and \(3\):

\[ 7 - 3 = 4 \]

Substitute back the result:

\[ (7 - 3) \sqrt{3} = 4 \sqrt{3} \]

Answer:

The simplest form of \(7 \sqrt{3} - 3 \sqrt{3}\) is:

\[ 4 \sqrt{3} \]
