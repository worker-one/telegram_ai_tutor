Solving the Differential Equation 2x' + 2x = 6

In this explanation, we will go through the step-by-step solution to the differential equation 2x' + 2x = 6.

Step 1: Write the differential equation

The given differential equation is:

$$2\frac{dx}{dt} + 2x = 6$$

Step 2: Divide both sides by 2

Divide both sides of the equation by 2 to simplify the coefficients:

$$\frac{dx}{dt} + x = 3$$

Step 3: Rearrange the equation to separate variables

Rearrange the equation to separate the variables x and t:

$$\frac{dx}{dt} = 3 - x$$

Step 4: Integrate both sides

Integrate both sides of the equation with respect to t:

$$\int \frac{dx}{3-x} = \int dt$$

Step 5: Evaluate the integrals

Evaluate the integrals on both sides:

$$-\ln|3-x| = t + C$$

Step 6: Solve for x

Solve for x by isolating the variable on one side of the equation:

$$x = 3 - Ce^{-t}$$

Conclusion

The general solution to the differential equation 2x' + 2x = 6 is x = 3 - Ce^{-t}, where C is the constant of integration.
